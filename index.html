Coffee contains a variety of compounds that contribute to its flavor and physiological effects, including caffeine, chlorogenic acid, tannins, fixed oils, carbohydrates, and proteins: Caffeine: A mild central nervous system stimulant that's also found in cocoa beans, kola nuts, and tea leaves. An 8-ounce cup of brewed coffee typically contains 80–100 milligrams (mg) of caffeine, while an 8-ounce cup of regular instant coffee contains about 62 mg. A single 1-ounce shot of espresso contains approximately 63 mg. Chlorogenic acid: May affect blood vessels and how the body handles blood sugar and metabolism. Studies have shown that coffee consumption can increase blood antioxidant levels. Tannins: Coffee contains 3–5% tannins
